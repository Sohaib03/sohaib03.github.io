Why We Need Specialized Vector Databases

@ Sohaib ~Apr 10, 2026 $1 min

With the rise of Large Language Models (LLMs) and dense embeddings, the ability to perform K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) searches at scale has become critical.

The Curse of Dimensionality

Traditional B-Trees and relational databases fail when dealing with high-dimensional vectors. As dimensions increase, the distance between any two points in the space becomes almost uniform. This phenomenon ruins the pruning capabilities of standard spatial indexes like R-Trees.

HNSW to the Rescue

Hierarchical Navigable Small World (HNSW) graphs have emerged as the gold standard for Approximate Nearest Neighbor (ANN) search. 1. HNSW creates a multi-layered graph mimicking skip-lists, allowing queries to funnel down from sparse to dense clusters. By constructing a meticulously layered graph, we can route queries logarithmically through the high-dimensional space without resorting to an O(N) brute-force scan.
