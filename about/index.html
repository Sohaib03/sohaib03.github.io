About Me

Welcome to my personal space! I’m a passionate educator, researcher, and developer with a deep love for problem-solving and teaching. My journey has been shaped by diverse experiences, from competitive programming to cutting-edge research, all driven by a desire to learn and share knowledge.

Background

I graduated with a BSc in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). Currently, I serve as an adjunct lecturer at BUET, teaching courses such as Software Engineering and Mathematical Analysis for Computer Science. My passion for teaching extends beyond the classroom, as I actively mentor aspiring programmers and help organize contests to inspire students.

Research Interests

My primary research interests lie in database systems, distributed systems, and bioinformatics. I’ve worked on improving the scalability of algorithms, focusing on reducing gene tree estimation errors, with papers under review at prestigious conferences like RECOMB.

Competitive Programming

As a Codeforces Master and a former ICPC regional finalist, competitive programming has been an integral part of my growth. It has honed my skills in writing efficient code, designing algorithms, and selecting optimal data structures for complex problems.

Personal Philosophy

Coming from an underprivileged background, I understand the challenges of accessing quality education. This fuels my drive to empower others, especially underrepresented groups, by providing guidance and creating opportunities for growth.

Hobbies

  • Teaching and mentoring students
  • Exploring scalable system designs
  • Developing tools and algorithms in C++, Python, and Golang
  • Reading about advancements in AI and distributed computing
