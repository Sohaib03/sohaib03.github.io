QT-WEAVER

Nov. 11, 2024

Overview

Summarizing individual gene trees into species phylogenies using coalescent-based methods has become a standard approach in phylogenomics. However, gene tree estimation error (GTEE)—arising from both analytical factors and biological causes (e.g., short gene sequences)—can adversely impact the accuracy of phylogenomic inference.QT-WEAVER introduces the novel problem of correcting the quartet distribution induced by a set of estimated gene trees.

This involves updating the weights of the quartets to better reflect their relative importance within the gene tree distribution. QT-WEAVER learns conflicts within the quartet distribution induced by a set of gene trees and adjusts the weights to generate an updated quartet distribution.

Advancements

  • General-Purpose Technique: QT-WEAVER does not require explicit modeling of the subject system or the sources of GTEE or gene tree heterogeneity.
  • Improved Species Tree Accuracy: Experimental results on simulated and empirical datasets demonstrate QT-WEAVER’s ability to effectively account for GTEE, leading to significant improvements in species tree accuracy.
  • Algorithmic Innovations: Introduces the concept of quartet conflicts, benefiting a wide range of quartet-based computations.
  • Open-Source Availability: Freely available for researchers and practitioners.
