WQFM-tree

Jul. 31, 2024

Overview

wQFM-TREE is a summary method developed for species tree estimation from multi-locus data, addressing gene tree discordance. It builds on the wQFM method and improves its scalability, making it applicable to large datasets.

Key Features

  • Improved Scalability: Removes the need for explicit quartet generation and weighting, allowing efficient processing of large datasets with many taxa and genes.
  • Polytomy Handling: Unlike wQFM, wQFM-TREE can handle datasets with polytomies.
  • Accuracy: In simulations, wQFM-TREE matches or outperforms ASTRAL in terms of accuracy across various model conditions.
  • Real-World Application: Successfully re-analyzed the green plant dataset from the One Thousand Plant Transcriptomes Initiative, demonstrating its practical utility.
  • Algorithmic Advances: Introduces improvements in computational methods, benefiting various quartet-based computations in phylogenetic analyses.

Performance

Simulations show that wQFM-TREE outperforms ASTRAL in 25 out of 27 conditions tested, with significant improvements in 20 of these conditions. It demonstrates reliability and accuracy, even in challenging phylogenetic models involving 200-1000 taxa.

Applications

wQFM-TREE offers an effective alternative to existing methods, with proven scalability and accuracy in large datasets, advancing phylogenetic estimation.

Availability

wQFM-TREE is available as open-source software.
